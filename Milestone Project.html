History of the Solar Systems

The Solar System formed about 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant cloud of interstellar gas and dust, a process described by the widely accepted nebular hypothesis.

The Nebula Hypothesis

The nebular hypothesis explains that our solar system formed from a giant, rotating cloud of gas and dust (a nebula) that collapsed under its own gravity, flattening into a spinning disk with the Sun forming at the center, while leftover material clumped together in orbits to form the planets, moons, and asteroids, with inner rocky planets and outer gas giants emerging due to temperature differences and material availability.

The Nebula Model

The original idea by Kant and Laplace has evolved into the Solar Nebular Disk Model (SNDM), incorporating more physics to explain the complex processes of planet formation (like viscosity and planetesimal growth).
